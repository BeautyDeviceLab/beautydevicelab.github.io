How to choose a microcurrent device (simple)

1) Conductive gel/serum is required

Microcurrent needs conductivity and slip. Using it on dry skin is uncomfortable and not recommended.

2) Short sessions beat long sessions

Most people get better consistency with 5-minute sessions than with longer routines.

3) Expect “subtle” improvements

Think tone and sculpt, not dramatic overnight transformation. The benefit compounds over weeks.

FAQ

How often should I use microcurrent?

Most routines rely on frequent short sessions early on, then maintenance. Follow your device instructions.

Do I really need conductive gel?

Yes. It improves comfort and helps the device glide. Avoid dry-skin sessions.

Can I combine microcurrent with LED?

Many people do. Start slowly, separate sessions if needed, and stop if skin becomes irritated.

Is microcurrent safe?

Follow the device guidance. If you have implanted devices or medical conditions, consult a professional.
