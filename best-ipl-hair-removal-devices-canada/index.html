How to choose an IPL device (Canada notes)

Skin tone & hair colour matter

IPL works best when there’s contrast between hair and skin. If you’re unsure, follow the manufacturer skin-tone chart. When in doubt, go conservative on intensity and patch-test first.

Comfort drives consistency

Devices with cooling-focused designs can feel easier to stick with. If you tend to quit routines, prioritize comfort and speed over “max power.”

Routine timeline

Expect weekly sessions early on, then maintenance. Budget devices can still work, but the timeline may feel slower.

Canada practicals

Standard Canadian outlets (120V) are typical. Warranty/returns depend on the seller listing. Always check who ships & sells.

Important: We’re not a medical provider. IPL isn’t appropriate for everyone, and eye safety matters. Read the official product instructions and warnings carefully.

FAQ

How long until you see results?

Most routines are measured in weeks, not days. Expect early reduction, then maintenance. The key is staying consistent with the schedule.

Does IPL hurt?

It varies. Many people describe it as a quick warm snap. Cooling-focused designs can feel easier for sensitive areas.

Can I use IPL on my face?

Some devices allow facial use below the cheekbone, others restrict it. Always follow the manufacturer instructions for face safety and intensity.

IPL vs electrolysis (ePen)?

IPL is better for larger areas. Electrolysis-style tools are more precise for single hairs or tiny zones. Many people use both: IPL for “coverage” and a precision tool for details.
