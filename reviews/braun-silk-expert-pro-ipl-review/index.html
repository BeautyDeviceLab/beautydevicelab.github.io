What the routine feels like (real-world)

Think “weekly appointment you do at home.” The device only wins if it becomes boring in the best way: a repeatable habit. Most people do best when they schedule a consistent day, keep the device accessible, and treat small areas first.

Session pace

Shorter, more frequent sessions early on are easier than marathon sessions. Start with one area and expand once your routine sticks.

Skin prep

Clean, dry skin helps. Many users prefer to shave (not wax) before IPL sessions. Follow the manufacturer instructions for timing.

Consistency

Results come from repetition. Missing weeks early on usually slows momentum more than choosing the “perfect” model.

Maintenance

After the initial phase, most routines shift to occasional maintenance sessions. This is where premium devices feel worth it.

Pro tip: If you’re overwhelmed, choose one “anchor area” (like underarms) and build from there. Small wins create the habit.

Comfort, sensitivity, and how to avoid regret

IPL comfort varies. Many describe it as a quick warm snap. If you’re sensitive, the strategy is not “max power,” it’s “max consistency without dread.”

Start conservative

Patch-test and step up gradually. Your routine should feel sustainable, not heroic.

Avoid rushing

Moving too fast or repeating flashes unnecessarily can make sessions feel harsher.

Sensitive areas

Underarms and bikini areas often feel more intense. Treat those areas when you’re calm and have time.

Follow official guidance

Always follow the included safety instructions and the skin-tone compatibility chart.

FAQ

Is this worth it vs salon visits?

For many routine users, yes. The value is highest when you do full-body areas and keep up with maintenance. If you only treat a tiny area, a budget device can make more sense.

How soon will I notice a difference?

IPL is a weeks-to-months process. Early reduction can appear sooner, but the real payoff is in sustained consistency.

What if I have sensitive skin?

Start lower, patch-test, and prioritize comfort. If discomfort makes you avoid sessions, a comfort-focused model may be better even if it’s less “premium.”

Should I choose Braun or Ulike?

Braun is the premium “reliability” pick. Ulike is often the “comfort first” value pick. If you tend to quit routines, comfort usually wins.
